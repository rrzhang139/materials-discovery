The Impossible Search

A crystal material is atoms arranged in a repeating 3D lattice. There are roughly 1060 possible arrangements — more than atoms in the observable universe.

99.9%
Unstable / Useless
~0.001%
Stable & Useful

And computing the properties of just one takes hours on a supercomputer.

What is a Crystal?

LATTICE
3 lengths (a, b, c) + 3 angles (α, β, γ) define the repeating box
ATOMS
Element type + position inside the box. These are the local inputs.
PROPERTIES
Band gap, energy, conductivity — global quantities that emerge from the whole infinite structure
